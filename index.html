Use Text-to-Speech to Read Documents Aloud

Do you find you need to read the same document again and again before you can absorb the information?
Text-to-speech makes it easier to absorb information when you are reading.
You can see a quick example of text-to-speech here. You can use text to speech on your laptop, smartphone, or tablet. You can use text-to-speech to read out loud any kind of document: Powerpoint slides, PDFs, websites, Word documents, even books or handouts.
Click here for more information on using text-to-speech.

Use Audio Note Taker to Record your Lectures

I'm happy to confirm that you have been approved to try Audio Note Taker.

How Audio Note Taker Works

Audio Note Taker visualizes the lecture recording as bars that you can highlight and edit. You can use Audio Note Taker to audio-record lectures while at the same time typing short notes. It allows you to quickly revisit parts of the lecture that you might want to listen to again.

Using Voice Recognition to Dictate

I'm happy to confirm that you have been recommended to try voice recognition.

You can find an introduction to voice recognition here, with links to using voice recognition on your laptop or smartphone.

Voice recognition is an easy way to get your ideas down into a document, so it's a good way to get started on an essay. It's also much quicker than typing.

A lot of people find it more natural to express themselves by speaking rather than by typing. If this is you, voice recognition is well worth trying!
